Lumpia Recipe

Crispy, simple, and delicious. A classic Filipino snack or side dish.

Recipe by: Arnaldo Posadas

Prep:   20 minutes    Cook:   10 minutes    Total:   30 minutes

Instructions

    Prep

  1. Finely dice the carrots, celery, and cabbage. Set aside in separate bowls.
  2. Add salt to the cabbage and mix.
  3. Set aside for 10-15 minutes. Afterwards, squeeze the cabbage in handfuls and drain the excess water.
  4. In a large bowl, add the ground pork.
  5. Add in all the veggies, sesame oil, egg, cornstarch, and seasonings.
  6. Mix thoroughly until well incorporated
  7. Place the finished filling into a large piping bag.
  8. Open the eggroll wrapper package and place a stack of them on a plate.
    Pro tip! Cover with a damp paper towel to prevent drying.

    9. Wrapping the lumpia

  9. Set up your wrapping station. A cutting board or flat surface to roll the lumpia, a small bowl of water with cornstarch for sealing the lumpia, the piping bag with the lumpia filling, the plate with the wrappers, and a plate or tray to place the finished lumpia in.
  10. Place a single eggroll wrapper down. Pipe a line of the filling on the bottom edge closest to you. Start rolling the wrapper up and away from you to form a tube-like shape.
  11. Before reaching the edge, brush the cornstarch water along the top edge to seal the wrapper.
  12. Repeat until desired number of lumpia is made or until ingredients run out.
    Extra lumpia can be frozen and stored for up to 2 months

    13. Cooking

  13. Prep a high-walled pot with oil to deep fry.
  14. Heat the oil to medium heat.
  15. Deep fry the lumpia for 10 minutes until golden brown and the filling is cooked through
  16. Serve and enjoy! You can eat them plain, with sweet and sour sauce, or sweet chili sauce.
